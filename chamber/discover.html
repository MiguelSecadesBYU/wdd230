Botanical Garden

The Atlantic Botanical Garden is a plant museum inaugurated in April 2003, conceived to study, conserve and publicize the flora and vegetation of the North Atlantic. It is the first Botanical Garden in the northwest of the Iberian Peninsula and stands out for its approach, modern and non-generalist, based on specialized collections of flora. Its lines of work focus on the promotion of activities and services, in the scientific, educational, tourist, and cultural fields, aimed at the conservation and dissemination of plant and natural heritage.

Initially conceived as a mining orphanage and transformed at the time of its construction into a Labor University, Luis Moya's building was conceived as a great self-sufficient ideal city closed in on itself, which even had a 100-hectare farm and was prepared for train generations of children of workers as highly qualified professionals.

With 270,000 square meters, the Universidad Laboral is the largest building in Spain and one of the most visited places in Gij√≥n.
